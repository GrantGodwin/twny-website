## 6. Market patterns to AVOID

**[INTERPRETATION / RECOMMENDATION]**
1. **Slogan-led heroes with no substance** ("Made Easy," "Transformational Conversations," "We're on IT") — pleasant, forgettable, unprovable.
2. **Universal fit** ("businesses of any size," "any industry") — signals no point of view.
3. **Commodity pricing cards on specs/ticks** (RAM, storage, feature grids) — invites price comparison, erodes margin and premium perception.
4. **Logo walls and badge soup** as a substitute for a clear capability story.
5. **Stock-photo "happy people at laptops"** and app-icon collages — visually interchangeable across the whole sector.
6. **Dark-SaaS gradient template** (the AI-consultant default) — already a saturated look by 2026.
7. **"Free strategy call" funnels with hidden pricing** — opposite of the TWNY transparency stance.
8. **Compliance-acronym scatter** (HIPAA/GDPR/PCI/ISO) with nothing behind it.

## 7. Market patterns worth borrowing — carefully

**[RECOMMENDATION]**
1. **Transparent, fixed, productised pricing with ranges and timeframes** (Source Digital) — aligns with "publish all, prominently."
2. **Explicit "no scope creep / no hourly surprise" language** — TWNY already has the substance (inclusions/exclusions, consulting as a separate line); say it plainly.
3. **A pricing FAQ** that pre-answers objections (annual vs monthly, GST, what's included).
4. **A named, credible human** with a real background (Atarix leans on people; TWNY can do this better with a genuine Modern Workplace operator).
5. **"Book a short call" as the single primary CTA** — but framed as the Atlas recommendation handover ("we'll recommend a starting point"), not a high-pressure funnel.
6. **A simple "how it works" sequence** — TWNY's operational loop (Discover → Recommend → Quote → Deliver → Operate → Review) is a stronger, ownable version of the generic "how it works" strip.

## 8. Common MSP clichés (to beat)
**[INTERPRETATION]** "Your trusted IT partner"; "proactive, not reactive"; "we take the hassle out of IT"; "fit for purpose solutions"; "people-first / technology-led"; "24/7 peace of mind"; "scalable solutions"; "let us be your virtual CIO"; hero photo of a smiling helpdesk headset; trust = vendor badge wall.

## 9. Common AI-consultant clichés (to beat)
**[INTERPRETATION]** "Unlock the power of AI"; "AI that actually works"; "future-proof your business"; "10x your productivity"; dark UI + neon-gradient + animated nodes/orbs; vague "AI agents" with no concrete task; "book a free AI audit"; fake urgency ("don't get left behind"); ROI numbers with no basis.

## 10. Common web-agency / hosting clichés (to beat)
**[INTERPRETATION]** "Give your business an online presence"; "stunning, responsive websites"; hosting sold on RAM/storage/"unlimited bandwidth"; "99.9% uptime" as the headline value; SSL and backups as nickel-and-dime upsells; "DIY builder + we host it"; portfolio carousels with no outcomes; "from $X/mo" race-to-the-bottom anchors.

## 11. How TWNY can avoid looking generic

**[RECOMMENDATION]** The market is generic in four predictable ways — TWNY's existing brand system already counters each:
1. **Voice:** replace slogans with the "decision + evidence + action" register and plain-English glosses. Say specific true things ("We run the Microsoft 365 tenant, the DNS, the backup, and the website — one source of truth") instead of "Made Easy."
2. **Visual:** reject teal-clipart, purple-gradient, and dark-SaaS templates for the restrained six-colour system (Ink/Steel/Mineral/Ember), wordmark-only, no stock photos, no icon collages, structure-from-the-operational-loop. (See [visual-reference-notes.md](./visual-reference-notes.md).)
3. **Commercial:** publish real, premium, two-mode pricing with explicit boundaries — the opposite of both the commodity floor and the hidden-pricing funnel.
4. **Point of view:** name the segment TWNY is *for* and the work it *won't* do. A site that turns the wrong customer away reads as confident and premium; "any size, any industry" reads as a template.

**The single biggest differentiator available:** every competitor is either *cheap-and-generic* (VentraIP, current TWNY) or *warm-but-vague* (Atarix) or *transparent-but-template* (Source Digital). **None is simultaneously premium, specific, transparent, and operator-credible.** That intersection is open, and the Atlas brand/commercial system is already built for it.
